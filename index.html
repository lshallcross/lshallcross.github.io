International Health Expenditures

Bubble Chart

Mental Health Spending

This bubble chart shows the amount of spending each U.S. state/territory spent on mental health, per capita, in 2010. The District of Columbia came in with the highest amount, at $361, and Puerto Rico came in with the lowest, at $23. The data comes from the Kaiser Family Foundation and the National Association of State Mental health Program Directors Research Institute.

Small Multiples Chart

How Californians Get Health Insurance

This small multiples chart shows where Californians' health insurance came from in 2013. Forty-five percent of Californians get health insurance from their employer, a combined total of 31 percent are on Medicaid and Medicare. The data comes from the Kaiser Family Foundation and the U.S. Census Bureau.
